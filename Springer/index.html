Updates to LNCS formats

Springer have updated what they want for LNCS formats. The main change is with the use of ORCIDs ORCID is an open standard which helps one to disambiguate homonym authors or merge variations of authors' academic names. Springer expects ORCID to become the standard unique author ID for research evaluation, funder reports, etc.

Spinger encourages authors to provide their ORCIDs when submitting their papers. If you do not have an ORCID you can apply for one at http://www.orcid.org/register. You then link to your ORCID using the header information in the new LNCS class file as follows 

\author{Ivar Ekeland\inst{1}\orcidID{0000-1111-2222-3333} 
\and
Roger~Temam\inst{2}\orcidID{1111-2222-3333-4444} 
\and
Jeffrey~Dean\orcidID{2222-3333-4444-5555} 
\and
David~Grove\orcidID{3333-4444-5555-6666} 
\and
Craig~Chambers\orcidID{4444-5555-6666-7777} 
\and
Kim~B.~Bruce\orcidID{5555-6666-7777-8888} 
\and
Elsa~Bertino\orcidID{6666-7777-8888-9999}}

Reminder

For a while Springer have been putting Appendices BEFORE bibliographies. Please ensure you do this, in fact probably best not to call them Appendices, and just have them as sections to avoid confusion. If you submit a file to Springer with an Appendix AFTER the bibliography, they will move it for the final version.
